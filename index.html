What is this game all about?

Coders and Decoders is a game for children ages 4 to 6. It was designed by a lover of chocolate and a lover of dinosaur nuggets as a fun way to to train various aspects of children's spatial thinking (e.g. spatial language, visuospatial perception) as well as their computational thinking skills.

How To Play

  1. Assign a player to be the starting coder, remainder of the players will be decoders. * Players are free to rotate, and take turns being the coder *
  2. The coder will choose a 3/4/5 letter word card and look at the word in secret. Decoders are to face away from the screen or cover their eyes during this part
  3. The coder will send out their algorithm for the decoders to process.
  4. Decoders will process the algorithm and either write, draw (or both!) the code word
